Rationale

My internet artwork is heavily imspired by the pieces,"Blind Spot" by Ying, and "keeping up appearances" by Obadike. Throughout this semester I have chosen to tackle the subject of decolonisation and to an extent, there is this belief that one should decolonise themselves. Do your own research, find your own history -figure it out yourself.

The piece I have create, "The Decolonial Dictionary" is actually a bit of an attempt and satire at this. By using my own essay as the work, and linking key terms to the definition pages in the Urban dictionary - I'm showcasing a bit of that research (but with a specific work that was an actual way in which I actually showcases a part of my decolonising my mentality journey), and a bit of how easy it is for that journey to be exhastive with the kind of content you come across and the people who dont take it seriously and the mass opinions that are uneducated. I'll admit sometimes it's funny too.

This piece takes format from Obadike and influence from Ying and uniquely expresses a current temporal space where the internet is good and bad and the world is pretty bad too, but people are trying to just, be. The piece isactually meant to be more educational, but I can't help the though of what an artifact this could become as contexts and definitions change - but the base work sticks it to right now.

It was a lot of fun to make and it was a bit tiring, but I am proud of it.
