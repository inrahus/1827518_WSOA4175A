Artistic Reflection 3

With my Internet Artwork, I spent a lot of time wondering what I really wanted to do with it. In thelast reflection, I express the roots of my project, and the topic of decoloniality that I wanted to deal with. I also, quite grandly, also agreed and wanted to implement the feedback I got, and diversify my idea across my site and let it be used with each of my essays. However, I felt that this really took away from what I was trying to achieve.

The reason I focused specifically onmy decoloniality essay for this work in the first place, was because i though it would be interesting to specifically explore theterms to do with the topic, in a work that fully addressed the topic. I had thought of doign it with the other essays of mine beofre the second submission but did not feel as strogly about the way that the topic branched out from this essay (well, except for "Blackness for Sale" to be honest). I felt that the tewrms I wanted to showcase did not appear as much in my other work as I used it more underlyingly than overarchingly.

With this submission, I decided to keep the content of my work from the previous submission. However, I changed the layout of my site, and the positioning of the work on the site to maximise the effect. Absically, at first site you are just presented with these terms and the title of the work. You can hover and click and follow,and at some point you realise you can highlight, but the text is too small because, as much as this essay was important, the understanding of the terms -and the use and emphasis here is on the influence of Urban Dictionary. This part of the site, the work, is also the only text that is not black, and noted to the fact that it is interactable and important as well.

I also changed the layout of the site to black and white to keep this effect and minimalised the work, as I mention in my Technical Reflection 3, to further this minimalist theme. This was an active decision I made after engaging with the work "keeping up appearances" and noted the effectof the minimalism on the work, as well as what the seperation and isolation of the work would do to benefit it.

in my original site, which I have kept to show for reference, I had this work laid out in the background - making it a chance interaction unless you got to a certain point on the hoomepage. Here, it is for the user to see and engage with-it is there and they cannot escape it. I also had several echnical issues with responsivity because of this layout, and in terms of this, this way of presenting my work was beneficial in more than one way.

As it is with any artwork, it can neverreally be comeplete, and while I am satisfied with my work and presentation and the way it pays tribute to the influences of Ying and Obadike, I am sure there is much to criticise and more to want. However, that exploration would be great for another time or project, and I am happy with the way my work comes across.

Thank you for the opportunity to explore this side of the internet.
